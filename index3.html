First person

Name: Nicholas Goepper

Skiing type: Freestyle

Informations: Nicholas Charles Goepper (born March 14, 1994) is an American freestyle skier. Representing the United States of America, Goepper won a bronze medal at the 2014 Winter Olympics in Sochi, Russia and won a silver medal at the 2018 Winter Olympics in Pyeongchang, South Korea.[4] He has also won three gold medals and two silver medals at the Winter X Games.

Second person

Name: Simon Dumont

Skiing type: Freestyle

Informations: Dumont is known for his exceptional amplitude in half-pipe competitions, regularly reaching heights up to 20 feet out of the pipe, and for his signature big-air trick, the superman double-frontflip. In 2006-07, Dumont overshot a 100-foot jump in Park City, Utah, by some eighty feet, landing flat, rupturing his spleen and breaking his pelvis in three places, but he returned to skiing only two months later.[citation needed] In 2009 he won the X Games Big Air competition by landing a double-frontflip. On April 11, 2008, Simon Dumont set the current world quarter-pipe height record at Sunday River in Bethel, Maine. He aired 35.5 feet out of a 38-foot quarter-pipe, while performing a cork 900 tail grab.

She won four World Cup overall championships—one of only two female skiers to do so, along with Annemarie Moser-Pröll—with three consecutive titles in 2008, 2009, and 2010, plus another in 2012. Fourth person

Name: Candide Thovex

Skiing type: Freestyle

Informations: In 1997 Candide signed his first professional sponsorship contract with leading Australian action sports brand Quiksilver. In 2004 Candide continued to push back the boundaries of his sport, performing the first ever ‘810° to rail’ and cementing his reputation as the master of all ‘new school’ tricks and techniques. Candide Thovex(born 22 May 1982) is a French professional skier, filmmaker and entrepreneur. He is notable for his creative freestyle skiing movies and professional accomplishments. He had an accident in 2007 so he stopped championships.
